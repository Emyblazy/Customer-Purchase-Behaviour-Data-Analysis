# Customer-Purchase-Behaviour-Data-Analysis
## Project Description
I analyzed customer purchase behaviour across 3,900+ e-commerce customers to uncover insights on key revenue drivers, customer segments, and product performance trends. Leveraged SQL and Ms Excel for data exploration and analysis to generate actionable insights. Additionally, Power Bi tool helped to develop a dashboard depicting the insights discovered.

## KPI QUESTIONS
1. Who are our most valuable customers?
•	What is the Age group and Gender distribution? 
•	Which age group and gender spends the most? 
•	Who are our repeat/high-frequency buyers?

2. What products drive the most revenue?
•	Which categories generate the highest sales? 
•	What product attributes (size, color) are most popular? 
•	Are there seasonal trends affecting sales performance? 

3. How do customers purchase?
•	What is the average purchase amount? 
•	How often do customers buy (frequency)? 
•	What payment methods are most used? 
	
4. What impact do discounts and promo codes have?
•	Does offering discounts significantly increase purchase frequency or amount? 
•	Are promo users more valuable or just one-time buyers?

## INSIGHTS
•	Customer purchases showed a quarterly concentration (29.41%), indicating periodic spikes in buying activity. Among the seasons, Winter recorded the highest purchase volume, suggesting strong seasonal influence on sales. 
•	Medium-sized products dominated demand, which accounted for approximately 45% of customer preferences, making them the most preferred size category. 
•	The Clothing category led product performance, contributing 44.5% of total demand, significantly outperforming other categories. 
•	Male customers represented the majority (68%) of the customer base, compared to 32% female customers, indicating a gender imbalance in customer distribution. 
•	The 55+ age group accounted for the largest customer segment (30.2%), highlighting a strong presence of older consumers in purchasing activity. 
•	Discount and promo code usage remained relatively low, with 57% of customers not utilizing any promotional incentives, while only 43% engaged with discounts. 
•	There is no significant variation in payment method preferences, suggesting customers are flexible and no single method dominates usage. 
•	Customer location does not significantly influence purchase behavior, likely due to the nature of the platform being e-commerce-based, which reduces geographical limitations. 

## RECOMMENDATIONS
•	Implement seasonal marketing strategies aligned with quarterly trends, with particular emphasis on Winter campaigns, to capitalize on peak purchasing periods and maximize revenue. 
•	Develop and promote products targeted at female customers, as their average purchase value (1484.4) is comparable to males (1533.6) despite representing a smaller customer base. Increasing female customer acquisition presents a strong opportunity for revenue growth. 
•	Prioritize inventory expansion and marketing efforts in the Clothing category, given its dominant contribution to overall sales performance. 
•	Ensure consistent availability of medium-sized products, as they represent the highest demand segment among customers. 
•	Design targeted campaigns and product offerings that appeal to the 55+ age group, leveraging their position as the largest customer segment. 
•	Increase the effectiveness of promotional strategies by encouraging discount and promo code adoption, particularly to drive repeat purchases and improve customer engagement.
